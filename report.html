SA Coastal Flood Risk Viewer

Methodology Report & Data Quality Statement  |  Department for Environment and Water — Portfolio Project

1. Purpose

This project demonstrates the integration of coastal spatial data with downscaled climate projections to deliver data-driven flood risk insights for South Australia's coastline. It was developed as a portfolio project targeting the Climate Data Analyst – Coastal Hazards (PO2) role at the Department for Environment and Water (DEW).

The tool enables planners and decision-makers to visualise how different sea level rise scenarios affect the SA coast under two emissions pathways, supporting adaptation planning and coastal hazard assessment.

3. Methods

3.1 Data Ingestion & Quality Assurance

The SA coastline was downloaded programmatically from Natural Earth's 50m resolution land polygon dataset and clipped to South Australia's geographic extent (129°E–141°E, 38.5°S–26°S). All layers were reprojected to GDA2020 MGA Zone 54 (EPSG:7854) — the standard coordinate system for SA government spatial work. QA checks were performed for null geometries, invalid geometries (fixed using buffer(0)), and bounding box verification.

3.2 Flood Extent Modelling

Three sea level rise scenarios were modelled based on CSIRO (2022) projections for Australia:

ScenarioSLRYearPathwayInland Buffer
SLR_05m0.5m 2050SSP2-4.5 250m
SLR_10m1.0m 2070SSP2-4.5 500m
SLR_20m2.0m 2100SSP5-8.5 1000m
Modelling assumption: In the absence of a DEM-based inundation model, flood extents were approximated using a proxy inland buffer (1m SLR ≈ 500m inland buffer), representing a simplified worst-case planar inundation on low-lying coastal terrain. A DEM-based approach using ELVIS or Geoscience Australia's 1-second DEM would improve accuracy in a production context.

3.3 Risk Scoring

Each coastal flood zone was assigned a composite risk score (0–100) using two equally weighted components:

  • Proximity score (50%) — inverse of buffer distance; zones closer to the shoreline score higher
  • Scenario severity score (50%) — proportional to SLR magnitude; higher-emissions scenarios score higher

Risk bands were assigned as: Low (<25), Medium (25–50), High (50–75), Extreme (>75).

6. Limitations

  • Flood extents use a simplified proxy buffer — not a DEM-based hydrological inundation model
  • Natural Earth 50m coastline has ~50m positional accuracy — suitable for regional planning, not site-level assessment
  • Risk scoring does not yet incorporate population exposure, asset value, or ecosystem sensitivity layers
  • Storm surge, wave action, and extreme event compounding are not modelled in this version
